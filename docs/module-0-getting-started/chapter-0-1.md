# Chapter 0.1: Welcome & Course Overview

Welcome to the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics curriculum! This chapter introduces you to the fundamental concepts that will guide your learning journey over the next 13 weeks.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift from traditional Digital AI, which operates primarily in virtual environments, to embodied intelligence that interacts with the physical world. Unlike Digital AI systems that process text, images, or data in isolation, Physical AI systems must navigate the complexities of real-world physics, sensorimotor grounding, and embodiment.

### The Embodiment Hypothesis

The embodiment hypothesis suggests that intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment. Rather than processing abstract symbols, Physical AI systems learn through sensorimotor experience, developing world models grounded in physical reality. This approach has proven essential for creating robots that can operate effectively in unstructured, dynamic environments.

### Sensorimotor Grounding

Traditional AI systems often suffer from the "symbol grounding problem" - the challenge of connecting abstract symbols to real-world meaning. Physical AI systems overcome this by grounding their understanding in sensory input and motor output, creating representations that are inherently tied to physical reality.

## Course Structure Overview

This 13-week curriculum is structured around 5 progressive modules that build upon each other:

1. **Module 0: Getting Started** (Weeks 1-2) - Course orientation and development environment setup
2. **Module 1: ROS 2 Fundamentals** (Weeks 3-5) - The robotic nervous system and communication patterns
3. **Module 2: Digital Twin** (Weeks 6-7) - Physics-accurate simulation for rapid prototyping
4. **Module 3: AI-Robot Brain** (Weeks 8-10) - GPU-accelerated perception, navigation, and learning
5. **Module 4: Vision-Language-Action Models** (Weeks 11-12) - Natural language control and multimodal interaction
6. **Module 5: Capstone Project** (Weeks 13-14) - Autonomous humanoid system integration

### Module Dependencies

The curriculum follows a logical progression where each module builds on the previous one:

- Module 1 (ROS 2) provides the communication foundation for all subsequent modules
- Module 2 (Simulation) allows safe testing of algorithms before hardware deployment
- Module 3 (Isaac) leverages simulation for AI training and deployment
- Module 4 (VLA) integrates AI capabilities with robotic systems
- Module 5 (Capstone) synthesizes all concepts into a working system

## Learning Path: From Simulation to Reality

The curriculum follows a "simulation-first" approach, where you'll develop and test algorithms in virtual environments before deploying them to physical hardware. This approach offers several advantages:

- **Safety**: Test algorithms without risk of hardware damage
- **Speed**: Rapid iteration without physical setup time
- **Cost**: Reduced hardware requirements during development
- **Reproducibility**: Consistent testing environments

However, we also emphasize the "sim-to-real gap" - the differences between simulated and real environments that must be addressed for successful deployment.
